I make photographs that sit with people, not just look at them. Presence over performance. Always.

Background

Gbadamosi Omotoyosi is a photographer and visual artist based in Nigeria. His work began through attention—to people, to environments, and to the quiet details that often go unnoticed. He works across portraiture and documentary photography, approaching each image as an exercise in observation and restraint.

Practice

His work moves between editorial portraiture, cultural documentation, and conceptual photography, consistently grounded in people and lived environments. Projects such as the Feed the Streets series, Fẹ́yì, Adé Ìbílẹ̀, Repose, and Memo from Yesterday reflect a practice shaped by observation—of identity, memory, and the everyday conditions people move through. Rather than staging moments, his work engages what is already present: how people carry themselves, how spaces hold emotion, and what remains after a moment passes.
